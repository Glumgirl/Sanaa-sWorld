About

I’m Sanaa Asim, a multilingual designer/researcher who loves thinking about place and space based in the UK.

As a designer, I’m interested in the ontological relationship between place and identity, and how that can be acknowledged and incorporated into the co-design process.

Other than that, I spend my time reading, doing embroidery, dancing and whatever my latest hobby is (right now it’s letter writing!)

Some things I’ve been thinking about are: Hyperlinks, the meaning of “sacred”, neocities, online MMPORGs and gut microbes.

I’m currently reading:

  • Braiding Sweetgrass by Robin Wall Kimmerer
  • Te Magazine No. 2 - Song of the Nightingale
  • I went to the waterfall by Baha Tahir
  • The Qur'an, translated by Abdul Hameed
  • + others
